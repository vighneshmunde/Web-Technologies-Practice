Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 for being "the jewel of Islamic art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage". It is regarded as one of the best examples of Mughal architecture and a symbol of Indian history. The Taj Mahal is a major tourist attraction and attracts more than five million visitors a year. In 2007, it was declared a winner of the New 7 Wonders of the World initiative. The Taj Mahal and its setting, surrounding grounds, and structures are a Monument of National Importance, administered by the Archaeological Survey of India.


Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in the city of Delhi in India. It was the main residence of the emperors of the Mughal dynasty for nearly 200 years, until 1857. It is located in the center of Delhi and houses a number of museums. In 2007, it was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.


Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a minaret and "victory tower" that forms part of the Qutub complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Mehrauli area of Delhi, India. The minaret was begun in 1192 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, the founder of the Delhi Sultanate, and was completed by his successor Iltutmish. It is made of red sandstone and marble, and is covered with intricate carvings and verses from the Quran. The Qutub Minar is one of the tallest minarets in the world, standing at 73 meters (240 feet) tall.


Kerala Backwaters

The Kerala Backwaters are a network of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and inlets that run parallel to the Arabian Sea coast of Kerala, India. They are a unique ecosystem that supports a wide variety of flora and fauna, and are an important source of livelihood for many local communities. The backwaters are also a popular tourist destination, known for their scenic beauty and tranquil atmosphere. Visitors can take boat rides through the backwaters, stay in traditional houseboats, and experience the local culture and cuisine.


Himalayas

The Himalayas, also known as the Himalaya Mountains, are a mountain range in Asia that separates the plains of the Indian subcontinent from the Tibetan Plateau. The range has many of the Earth's highest peaks, including the highest, Mount Everest. The Himalayas are home to some of the world's highest peaks, including Mount Everest, which stands at 8,848 meters (29,029 feet) above sea level. The range is also home to many other notable peaks, such as K2, Kangchenjunga, Lhotse, and Makalu. The Himalayas are a popular destination for mountaineers and trekkers from around the world.


Goa Beaches

Goa is a state in western India with coastlines stretching along the Arabian Sea. It is known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and Portuguese heritage. Some of the most popular beaches in Goa include Baga Beach, Anjuna Beach, Calangute Beach, and Palolem Beach. These beaches offer a range of activities such as water sports, beach parties, and relaxation. Goa's beaches are a major tourist attraction and draw visitors from around the world.


Varanasi Ghats

Varanasi, also known as Benares or Kashi, is a city in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and is considered a holy city in Hinduism. The city is famous for its ghats, which are a series of steps leading down to the banks of the River Ganges. The ghats are used for various religious rituals, including bathing, cremation, and prayer. Varanasi's ghats are a major tourist attraction and draw visitors from around the world who come to experience the city's unique culture and spirituality.


Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is a Sikh gurdwara located in the city of Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Sikhs and is known for its stunning architecture and spiritual significance. The temple is built around a man made pool that was completed by the fourth Sikh Guru, Guru Ram Das, in 1577. The temple's upper floors are covered in gold leaf, giving it its distinctive appearance. The Golden Temple is a symbol of equality and brotherhood, and it welcomes people of all faiths to visit and experience its beauty and spirituality.


Meenakshi Temple

The Meenakshi Temple, also known as the Meenakshi Amman Temple, is a historic Hindu temple located in the city of Madurai, Tamil Nadu, India. The temple is dedicated to the goddess Meenakshi, an avatar of the goddess Parvati, and her consort Sundareswarar, an avatar of the god Shiva. The temple is known for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and vibrant colors. It is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Hindus and attracts millions of visitors each year. The Meenakshi Temple is a symbol of Tamil culture and heritage and is considered one of the greatest architectural achievements in India.


Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is a historic palace located in the city of Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. It was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh and is known for its unique architecture and intricate design. The palace is made of red and pink sandstone and features a façade with 953 small windows, called jharokhas, which allow air to circulate through the palace and keep it cool during the hot summer months. The Hawa Mahal is a popular tourist attraction and is considered one of the most iconic landmarks in Jaipur.


City Palace, Jaipur

The City Palace in Jaipur, Rajasthan, India, is a magnificent complex of palaces, courtyards, and gardens that was built by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II in the 18th century. The palace is a blend of Rajasthani and Mughal architectural styles and is known for its intricate carvings, beautiful frescoes, and stunning courtyards. The City Palace is home to several museums that showcase the rich history and culture of Jaipur and Rajasthan. It is a popular tourist attraction and is considered one of the most important landmarks in Jaipur.


Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building located in Kolkata, West Bengal, India. It was built between 1906 and 1921 in memory of Queen Victoria, who was the Empress of India from 1876 until her death in 1901. The memorial is an iconic symbol of Kolkata and is known for its stunning architecture, which combines elements of British and Mughal styles. The Victoria Memorial houses a museum that contains a collection of artifacts, paintings, and sculptures related to the history of Kolkata and the British Raj in India. It is a popular tourist attraction and is considered one of the most important landmarks in Kolkata.


Jim Corbett National Park

Jim Corbett National Park is a protected area located in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand, India. It is named after Jim Corbett, a British hunter and conservationist who played a key role in the establishment of the park. The park is known for its rich biodiversity and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including Bengal tigers, leopards, elephants, and various species of birds. Jim Corbett National Park is a popular destination for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers, offering opportunities for safaris and wildlife photography.


Sundarbans National Park

Sundarbans National Park is a protected area located in the Sundarbans region of West Bengal, India. It is part of the larger Sundarbans mangrove forest, which is the largest mangrove forest in the world. The park is known for its unique ecosystem and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including the Bengal tiger, saltwater crocodile, and various species of birds and fish. Sundarbans National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is a popular destination for eco-tourism and wildlife enthusiasts.


Gir National Park

Gir National Park, also known as Sasan Gir, is a protected area located in the Junagadh district of Gujarat, India. It is the only place in the world where the Asiatic lion can be found in the wild. The park is known for its rich biodiversity and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including leopards, deer, antelopes, and various species of birds. Gir National Park is a popular destination for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers, offering opportunities for safaris and wildlife photography.


India Gate

India Gate is a war memorial located in New Delhi, India. It was designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens and was built to honor the Indian soldiers who died during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. The monument is an iconic symbol of India and is known for its impressive architecture and historical significance. India Gate is a popular tourist attraction and is often visited by locals and tourists alike to pay their respects to the fallen soldiers.


Marine Drive, Mumbai

Marine Drive is a famous promenade located in Mumbai, Maharashtra, India. It stretches along the Arabian Sea and is known for its stunning views of the coastline and the city skyline. Marine Drive is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike, offering a place to relax, take a stroll, and enjoy the sea breeze. The promenade is especially beautiful at sunset when the sky is painted with vibrant colors. Marine Drive is also home to several iconic landmarks, including the Art Deco buildings that line the street.


Cyber City, Gurgaon

Cyber City is a prominent business district located in Gurgaon, Haryana, India. It is known for its modern infrastructure, high-rise buildings, and vibrant commercial environment. Cyber City is home to numerous multinational corporations, IT companies, and business process outsourcing (BPO) firms. The area also features a variety of restaurants, cafes, and entertainment options, making it a popular destination for professionals and visitors alike. Cyber City has become a symbol of Gurgaon’s rapid urbanization and economic growth.
